# Copywriting

> Write marketing copy that's clear, specific, and built to convert — not impressive-sounding filler.

## What This Does

Gives Claude the mindset and framework of a senior conversion copywriter. It stops Claude from writing vague, generic marketing language and instead produces copy grounded in customer psychology, clear benefits, and specific outcomes.

## When to Use

- Writing or rewriting a homepage, landing page, pricing page, or feature page
- Crafting a hero section, headline, or CTA
- Translating product features into customer benefits
- You keep getting copy that sounds like "Streamline your workflow with next-gen solutions"

## Example Prompts

- "Write a hero section for my SaaS tool that helps freelancers track client payments"
- "My pricing page isn't converting. Rewrite it for a B2B analytics tool, $49/$99/$249 tiers"
- "Write a feature page for our AI email assistant. Target: busy sales reps"

---

## Core Philosophy

**Clarity beats cleverness.** If you have to choose, choose clear.

**Benefits, not features.** Features: what it does. Benefits: what that means for the customer. Always bridge the two.

**Specificity beats vagueness.**
- Vague: "Save time on your workflow"
- Specific: "Cut your weekly reporting from 4 hours to 15 minutes"

**Customer language, not company language.** Use the words your customers use. Mirror how they describe their own problem.

**One idea per section.** Each section advances one argument. Build a logical flow down the page.

---

## Before Writing

Gather this context. If the user hasn't provided it, ask for what's missing before writing:

**Page purpose**
- What type of page? (homepage, landing page, pricing, feature, about)
- What is the ONE primary action visitors should take?

**Audience**
- Who is the ideal customer?
- What problem are they trying to solve?
- What objections or hesitations do they have?
- What language do they use to describe their problem?

**Product**
- What are you selling or offering?
- What makes it different from alternatives?
- What's the key transformation or outcome?
- Any proof points? (numbers, testimonials, case studies)

**Context**
- Where is traffic coming from? (ads, organic, email, word of mouth)
- What do visitors already know before they arrive?

Do not write until you have at least the page type, primary action, and target audience. Ask for what you need.

## Writing Rules

1. **Simple over complex** — "Use" not "utilize." "Help" not "facilitate."
2. **Specific over vague** — Avoid "streamline," "optimize," "innovative," "seamless."
3. **Active over passive** — "We generate reports" not "Reports are generated."
4. **Confident over qualified** — Remove "almost," "very," "really."
5. **Show over tell** — Describe the outcome instead of labeling it great.
6. **Honest over sensational** — No fabricated stats or testimonials.

**Banned phrases:** Elevate, Unleash, Next-Gen, Game-changer, Seamless, Delve, Tapestry, Cutting-edge, Revolutionary, Transformative, In the world of...

**Quick check before any output:**
- Jargon that would confuse an outsider?
- Sentences trying to do two things at once?
- Passive voice?
- Exclamation marks? (remove them)
- Marketing buzzwords without substance?

## Page Structure

### Above the Fold

**Headline — the single most important message**

Formula options:
- `{Achieve outcome} without {pain point}`
- `The {category} for {specific audience}`
- `Never {unpleasant event} again`
- `{Question that names their exact problem}`
- `{Bold claim} — {proof or qualifier}`

Rules: specific > generic, outcome > description, customer-focused > company-focused.

**Subheadline**
Expands on the headline. Adds specificity or addresses the next objection. Max 2 sentences.

**Primary CTA**
Communicate what they get, not what they do.
- Weak: Submit, Sign Up, Get Started, Learn More
- Strong: Start Free Trial, Get the Report, See It in Action, Create Your First [Thing]

Formula: `[Action Verb] + [What They Get] + [Qualifier if needed]`

---

### Core Page Sections

| Section | Purpose | Notes |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Social proof | Build immediate credibility | Logos, numbers, short testimonials |
| Problem/Pain | Show you understand their situation | Use their language, not yours |
| Solution/Benefits | Connect features to outcomes | 3–5 key benefits max |
| How It Works | Reduce perceived complexity | 3–4 steps, keep it simple |
| Objection Handling | FAQ, comparisons, guarantees | Address the real hesitations |
| Final CTA | Recap value, repeat ask, risk reversal | Lower the stakes to act |

---

### Page-Specific Notes

**Homepage**
Multiple audiences, broad entry points. Lead with the widest value proposition. Create clear paths for different visitor intents (e.g., a developer vs. a founder).

**Landing Page**
Single message. Single CTA. Everything on the page exists to support that one conversion. Match your headline to the ad or email that sent them here.

**Pricing Page**
Job: help visitors self-select the right plan. Make the recommended tier obvious. Answer "which is right for me?" before they have to ask.

**Feature Page**
Connect: `feature → benefit → outcome`. Show a use case, not just a description.

**About Page**
Tell why you exist, then connect that mission to the customer's benefit. Still needs a CTA.

## Output Format

For every copy request, deliver:

**1. Page Copy**
Organized by section with headers: Headline, Subheadline, CTA, then each section.

**2. Annotations**
For key decisions, explain the reasoning in brackets: `[Led with the problem because traffic is coming from search — visitors already know the category]`

**3. Alternatives**
For headlines and CTAs, always provide 3 options:
- Option A: [copy] — [rationale]
- Option B: [copy] — [rationale]
- Option C: [copy] — [rationale]

**4. Meta (if relevant)**
Page title and meta description for SEO.
